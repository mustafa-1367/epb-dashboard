Submit a New Citizen Proposal Recorded On-Chain

As a registered citizen, you can submit proposals for how public funds should be spent in your community. Each proposal is permanently recorded on the blockchain for full transparency and public audit.

Min: $1,000 - Max: $500,000 per proposal
Minimum age: 14 years (Estonian e-PB eligibility)
Your identity will be hidden but demographics preserved for statistics
Gas fee: ~0.002 MATIC
How It Works
Phase 1: Budget Determination
Government defines the total participatory budget and records it on-chain.
Phase 2: Idea Submission
Citizens (age 14+, verified residents) submit proposals. Each gets a unique project ID on-chain.
Phase 3: Public Discussion
Citizens discuss, like, and comment on proposals. All interactions recorded on-chain.
Phase 4: Expert Evaluation
Verified experts review feasibility and approve or reject with justification.
Phase 5: Citizen Voting (14 days)
All registered citizens vote on-chain. Each citizen gets one vote per proposal. Budget allocation votes determine funding priority.
Phase 6: Implementation
City council approves. Funds disbursed via smart contract. Progress tracked on-chain with milestones.
Phase 7: Feedback Gathering
Post-implementation citizen ratings (1-5) and comments recorded on-chain for accountability.
